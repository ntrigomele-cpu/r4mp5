Ramps benchmark — Argentina

A UX/product analysis of how EMCD compares against global CEX (Binance, Bitget, OKX, MEXC, BingX, Nexo) and regional LATAM crypto apps (Bitso, Belo, Lemon) in the Argentine market. Covering all ramp types — ARS, USD, crypto on/off — plus entry-point naming, flow quality, and strategic implications.

Research by UX & Product · June 2026 · 9 platforms benchmarked from live mobile apps

1Executive summary

The bottom line Coinhold is a new wallet, separated from EMCD mining, entering an Argentine market where local USD and ARS ramps have become table stakes. Binance, Nexo, Bitso, Belo and Lemon all offer ARS rails. Among platforms that compete via P2P (Binance, Bitget, OKX, MEXC, BingX, EMCD), EMCD is the only one with no ARS book liquidity. The product has real strengths — clean Spanish UI, 0% fee crypto transfers by email and phone, internal instant transfers, Payment Card — but they're hidden under a FIAT flow that dead-ends.

4What Coinhold gets right (and competitors don't)

Before the gaps, the strengths. These are real differentiators that the current home doesn't surface — but they exist in the product today.

Spanish UI Strength
"Depositar / Convertir / Transferir / Retirar" — the Spanish UI uses the right verbs. Cleaner naming than the English version, on par with Belo and Lemon. This is a strength for Argentine users on Spanish locale.
Crypto send · email + phone Strength
Send crypto by email or phone, 0% fee — "A cualquier persona, sin comisiones". This is what Bitso (Bitso Transfer), Belo and Lemon (Lemon Tag) charge a premium to keep proprietary. Coinhold has it, free.
Internal transfer Strength
Cuenta principal ↔ Cuenta de trading, instant, 0 fee. Quick percentage selectors (10/25/50/75/100%). Power-user feature that competes well with Binance's funding-to-spot transfer.
Deposit menu structure Strength
Clean categorization: DEPOSITAR CON CRIPTO, COMPRAR CON FIAT, TUS PRODUCTOS. Information architecture is sound — the problem is what sits inside "Comprar con FIAT" (P2P only, empty book).
Underused strength

Coinhold already has features the LATAM apps treat as core differentiators

Bitso Transfer, Lemon Tag, and Belo's contact-list sending are all headline features for those products. Coinhold offers the same thing — crypto send via email and phone at 0% fee — but it lives two taps deep inside "Transferir → Enviar cripto". A user landing on the home screen has no idea this exists.

Same story with the Payment Card and internal account-to-account transfers. The product is more capable than the home screen suggests.

5The naming problem — only in English

Initial relevamiento focused on the English UI and flagged "Top up" as a naming issue. The Spanish UI tells a different story: the labels are correct. But not all Argentine users default to Spanish — a meaningful share use the app in English.

EMCD in English Issue
"Top up / Converter / Transfer / Withdraw" — "Top up" reads as prepaid recharge in the Argentine market. "Converter" is not a standard English word (Convert is). The label set feels machine-translated.
EMCD in Spanish Correct
"Depositar / Convertir / Transferir / Retirar" — clean Spanish, matches Belo and Lemon. This is the version that works for Argentina.
Belo (reference) Benchmark
"Ingresar / Intercambiar / Transferir" — three primary circular CTAs plus the FX rate on the home screen. Sets the bar Argentine users now expect.
Quick win

Fix the English locale to match the Spanish version

The Spanish UI proves the team knows the right naming. The English UI looks like an early literal translation. "Top up" → "Deposit". "Converter" → "Convert". "Withdraw" stays. Same word count, same icons, just better English. This is a copy change, not a redesign.

6The empty P2P problem

Naming aside, the most damaging issue in EMCD today is the FIAT flow. Both deposit and withdraw routes for ARS lead to a P2P with zero offers. The infrastructure is built but the market is missing.

EMCD — Depositar menu Setup
"Comprar con FIAT" offers only A través de P2P. No bank transfer, no card, no QR. The Spanish naming is correct, but the destination is empty.
EMCD — Retirar menu Same dead end
"Retirar a FIAT" also routes only to A través de P2P. To withdraw ARS, the user must find a counterparty in the same empty market. Two flows, one bottleneck.
EMCD — P2P result Dead end
"No such offers available. Try applying different filters." The message blames the filters and offers no alternative path forward.
Binance — same flow Reference
Same screen, different platform. Dozens of active sellers, payment methods (Brubank, Uala, Naranja X, Mercadopago, Belo), price, limits, 15-minute completion windows.
Critical · structural

The dead end happens in both directions — deposit and withdraw

An Argentine user trying to deposit pesos hits the empty P2P. So does an Argentine user trying to withdraw pesos. The same broken market is the only path for both flows. This is more damaging than missing the feature entirely: a platform that doesn't claim ARS support keeps trust intact; a platform that shows ARS UI and then returns "No such offers available" loses it.

7The USD ramp battleground

Argentina has the highest USD-saving rate in Latin America. Freelancers earn in dollars (USDT, USDC, Wise, Deel, PayPal). The competitor offering the cleanest USD onboarding wins this user. ARQ built a business on this. Revolut is preparing to enter. Binance already moved.

Binance — USD deposit Best in class
Binance offers a local USD bank transfer inside Argentina (zero fees, up to 1 business day, credited as USDC). Plus card, SWIFT, Google Pay. Unique among global CEX: it lets Argentines deposit the USD they already hold domestically.
Nexo — USD account Reference
Nexo opens a personal USD account via Bridge: receive ACH and FedWire transfers from US banks, plus Wise, PayPal, Deel. The flow explicitly markets these income sources.
Nexo — USDx / EURx / GBPx Reference
Multi-currency: USDx with free transfers from $5,000, EURx from €100, GBPx from £100. Direct competition for freelancers who get paid in mixed currencies.
EMCD — Depositar menu Missing
EMCD's "Comprar con FIAT" section offers only P2P. No USD virtual account, no ACH/Wire receiving address, no way for an Argentine freelancer to land dollar income.
Strategic

USD ramps are the next competitive front in Argentina

The market is shifting from "deposit pesos, buy stablecoins" to "receive dollars directly, hold them, optionally swap to ARS when needed". This serves three of the largest user groups in the country:

1. Freelancers earning from US/EU clients via Wise, Deel, PayPal, direct ACH.
2. Dollar-savers moving funds from local USD bank accounts into yield-bearing accounts.
3. Stablecoin natives who want USDT/USDC with a US dollar reference.

Binance, Nexo, Bitso, Belo and Lemon all serve this user today. ARQ and similar fintechs are built for it. Revolut is preparing for it. EMCD currently does not address it.

8LATAM crypto apps set the UX bar

The three Argentine players (Bitso, Belo, Lemon) don't compete with EMCD in trading depth or asset variety — but they set the UX expectations Argentine users now bring to any crypto app. Three patterns are now table stakes locally:

Bitso — Receive Pattern 1
Conversational framing. "¿Qué querés recibir?" The CVU is right there, copyable. No abstraction layers.
Belo — Ingresar Pattern 2
Currency-first navigation. Pesos, Dólares, Reales (Pix), Euros — then digital platforms (Airtm, Payoneer), then stablecoins. The user picks what before how.
Lemon — Enviar Pattern 3
Full-screen action. "¿Qué querés enviar?" with the four options as large typography: Pesos, Dólar Digital, Reales via PIX, Crypto. Treats each as a first-class verb.
Bitso — Home FX visibility
Exchange rate on home. "Dólares digitales · Comprás a 1,512.65 ARS · Vendés a 1,482.87 ARS". Answers the most common Argentine question before the user has to ask.
UX patterns to adopt

Three patterns Argentine users now expect from any crypto app

1. Conversational, verb-first framing. "¿Qué querés depositar?", "¿Qué querés enviar?" — lowers cognitive load for non-trading users.

2. Currency-first information architecture. Pick the currency (ARS / USD / USDT / BTC) before the method. The opposite of CEX convention, but it matches how Argentines think.

3. The exchange rate is on the home screen. Visible without any taps. Buys credibility with the dollar-anxious Argentine user.

10Key findings for EMCD

Critical · structural

The empty P2P is the worst single moment in the product

EMCD's only ARS path — for both deposit and withdrawal — returns "No such offers available". Of the 6 platforms in this benchmark that offer P2P in Argentina, EMCD is the only one with no ARS book liquidity. The gap is not feature-level, it's market-making.

Strength · hidden

The Spanish UI is correctly named — the English isn't

"Depositar / Convertir / Transferir / Retirar" works for Argentine users on Spanish locale. The English version still says "Top up / Converter / Transfer / Withdraw", which reads as machine-translated. A meaningful share of Argentine users keep their phone in English, so the issue is partial but real.

Strength · underused

Coinhold already has neobank-grade transfer features, just buried

Send crypto by email (0% fee), send crypto by phone number (0% fee), instant internal transfers between accounts. These are the features Bitso, Belo and Lemon market as headline differentiators. Coinhold has them — but they live two taps deep, while the home screen leads with the broken FIAT flow.

Critical · USD ramp gap

No USD on-ramp for the freelancer segment

The most strategically important user segment in Argentina right now is the freelancer earning in USD. They need a place to receive ACH/Wire, hold USD without inflation risk, and optionally swap. Binance, Nexo, Bitso, Belo and Lemon all serve this user. ARQ exists for this user. Revolut is coming for this user. Coinhold does not currently address this user.

Strategic question

Coinhold must decide its FIAT positioning

Since Coinhold is now separated from EMCD mining and operates as a new wallet, it has the opportunity to define its FIAT identity from scratch. Two paths:

(a) Compete on local ramps. Partner with virtual-account providers (Bridge for USD, Ualá/Prex or similar for ARS CVU). This is what Binance, Bitso, Belo and Lemon did. Expensive, regulated, slow.

(b) Skip local ramps, double down on crypto-native flows. Lead with the strengths Coinhold already has — email/phone crypto send, internal transfers, Payment Card, yield. Communicate clearly that the user brings their own crypto. This is closer to what Bitget/OKX/MEXC do.

What's not viable is the current middle ground: showing a P2P UI that returns empty results.

11Recommendations for UX & Product

Three priority tiers based on impact and dependency, not timeline. "Now" means the change is unblocked and high-impact. "Next" means it requires a product or ops decision. "Strategic" means it requires a business-level commitment.

Now — unblocked, high impact

Now
Redesign the P2P empty state

Replace "No such offers available / Try applying different filters" with: "We're growing our P2P sellers in ARS. In the meantime, you can deposit crypto from another wallet, or get notified when sellers are active". Convert dead-end into preserved relationship.

Now
Fix the English locale to match the Spanish version

"Top up" → "Deposit". "Converter" → "Convert". The Spanish UI proves the team knows the right naming — bring the English version up to parity. Copy change, not redesign.

Now
Surface email/phone crypto send on the home

The 0% fee send by email/phone is buried two taps deep. Bitso, Belo and Lemon lead with this feature. Promote it to a home-level shortcut or card — instant differentiator with no engineering cost.

Now
Hierarchize the home actions

Today Depositar / Convertir / Transferir / Retirar have identical visual weight. Define a primary action (Deposit for new users) and demote the rest. Look at Binance's yellow CTA pattern.

Now
Surface the exchange rate on home

Show USDT/ARS (or BTC/ARS) buy/sell rates as a tappable home card. All three LATAM crypto apps do this. Even Binance does it. It answers the user's first question without forcing a tap.

Now
Promote Payment Card to a home action

Today it lives below the fold in a sidebar card. Argentine users who can't deposit ARS but already hold crypto would value an instant card top-up flow. Make it discoverable.

Next — product & ops decisions

Next
Build P2P market-maker incentives

BingX uses "Tarifa 0" as an explicit promotion. Binance has its sponsored merchants program. Without a market-maker acquisition strategy in ARS, P2P will stay empty. Liquidity precedes UX here.

Next
Adopt currency-first information architecture

Like Bitso and Belo: ask "What currency are you bringing?" before showing methods. Aligns with the dollar-anxious Argentine mental model and surfaces gaps gracefully ("USD not available yet" is better than "P2P with no offers").

Next
Integrate USD virtual accounts via Bridge or similar

Nexo, Bitso, Belo and Lemon all integrated this. The infrastructure exists. A USD ACH/Wire receiving address opens up the freelancer segment without local Argentine regulation work. Highest-ROI ramp investment.

Next
Add onboarding to the P2P flow

If P2P remains the FIAT path, copy OKX's pattern: a short welcome screen before exposing the marketplace. Reduces errors and frames expectations. Especially important when liquidity is thin.

Strategic — business decisions

Strategic
Decide: compete on local ramps or skip them

The middle ground (P2P-only with no liquidity) is the worst place to be. Either commit to ramp infrastructure (CVU partnership, Bridge for USD) or commit to crypto-native positioning and remove the broken P2P entry.

Strategic
Define Coinhold's wedge in Argentina

Coinhold is a new wallet separated from EMCD mining. With the brand still forming, the question is: who is the target Argentine user? Freelancer with USD income, dollar-saver, trader, payments user? Each implies a different ramp investment.

Strategic
Prepare for Revolut

Revolut is preparing its Argentine entry (currently active in Mexico, Peru, Colombia). It will arrive with multi-currency accounts, freelancer-grade USD onboarding, and a strong UX bar. Today Coinhold has neither rate visibility nor USD onboarding. The window to address this is now, not after Revolut launches.

Strategic
Watch Binance's local USD rail

Binance now lets Argentines deposit USD from their domestic USD bank account. Unprecedented for a global CEX, and addresses the deep cultural USD-saving habit. If it gains traction, the "deposit local USD" feature becomes a category expectation, not a Binance specialty.

12Conclusion

Coinhold's gap in Argentina is not a UX detail. It's a product strategy question wearing a UX disguise — but the product is stronger than the home screen suggests.

The strengths exist: clean Spanish UI, 0% fee email/phone crypto send, instant internal transfers, Payment Card. The competitors charge a premium to keep features like Bitso Transfer or Lemon Tag proprietary; Coinhold has them and hides them. Surface these and the perceived product changes overnight.

But the FIAT flow is broken in both directions. Deposit and withdraw both dead-end at the same empty P2P. The first-time Argentine user concludes Coinhold doesn't work locally and uninstalls before discovering the strengths.

The viable strategic answers are: (1) build CVU and USD ramps and compete head-on with Binance and Bitso, (2) seed P2P liquidity aggressively to make the existing flow work, or (3) declare crypto-native positioning, lead with email/phone send and Payment Card, and remove the misleading FIAT promise.

The current state — strong product hidden behind a broken FIAT promise — is the only option that's strictly worse than all three. That's the first decision Coinhold needs to make. Everything else follows from it.
